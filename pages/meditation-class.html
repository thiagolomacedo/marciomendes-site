Meditation: Meaning, Origins, and Benefits

What Is Meditation?

The word meditation comes from the Latin meditatio, meaning "to think," "to contemplate," or "to reflect." In its earliest use, it described deep thought or spiritual reflection. Over time, meditation evolved across many traditions — from ancient Hindu and Buddhist practices in India (as early as 1500 BCE) to Taoist, Jewish, Christian, and Stoic contemplative disciplines.

Today, meditation is recognized worldwide as a practical method to focus the mind, cultivate awareness, and nurture inner peace.

(Britannica, Etymology Online)

Why Practice Meditation?

Meditation invites us to pause, turn our attention inward, and connect consciously with the present moment. Whether you are a beginner or an experienced practitioner, meditation offers a powerful way to manage stress, improve mental focus, and deepen self-understanding.

Reduced Stress and Anxiety

Research shows that mindfulness meditation helps calm the nervous system and lower stress hormones.

Improved Emotional Well-Being

Regular practice supports emotional balance, increases self-awareness, and enhances mood regulation.

Meditation strengthens concentration and helps you stay centered amid daily distractions.

(Humanitas University)

About the Instructor

IMG MESTRE 1

Mestre Márcio Mendes

Mestre Márcio Mendes is much more than a capoeira master — he is an educator, thinker, and a catalyst for social change. With a deep passion for life and a strong sense of responsibility for the well-being of others, he has dedicated his journey to contributing to society through art, education, and critical thinking.

As the founder of TRECIRAL, an innovative technique that merges capoeira movements with deep stretching, emotology, meditation, and Afro-Brazilian dance, Mestre Márcio has positively impacted the lives of law enforcement professionals and individuals seeking to enhance their physical and psychological well-being.
